Overview

Our primary goal for this project was to create a novel interface that augments in-person and/or remote communication and collaboration. In pursuit of this objective and in response to the increasing trend of remote work among companies, we have developed an innovative interface aimed at recreating the essence of an in-person office environment. Our interface is designed to bridge the physical gap between remote employees, fostering a sense of connection and presence as if they were co-located in an office setting. Through this platform, employees can visualize and interact with their colleagues virtually, mimicking the experience of being in the same physical space.

Interface Logic

Our interface operates on a virtual office concept, providing users with a dynamic representation of a shared workspace. Employees can access this interface through a web or application-based platform. Initially, each employee must create a personalized account on CoWork Central, setting up their profile with their preferred name, pronouns, and selecting an avatar to represent them in the virtual workplace. Subsequently, they can log in using their name and company email.

The system utilizes real-time data to display the status of each individual within the virtual office – whether they are seated at their desks, in a meeting, on a lunch break, away from their station, or engaged in specific activities like using the washroom. When an employee is present at their office space, a flashing green light indicates their availability, while an absent or unavailable employee is indicated by a flashing red light near their office, denoting their unavailability.

Users navigate through this virtual office, represented as a map or floor plan, with the ability to move freely across the space. As employees move closer to each other within the virtual office, audio-visual communication tools are activated, allowing for seamless interaction. This proximity-based communication feature creates an organic and natural way for employees to engage in conversations or collaborate as if they were physically co-located.

Initial 10 Sketches

Each team member was asked to sketch out 10 ideas that they believe could be suitable for an innovative and novel interface. We then discussed each idea thoroughly and brainstormed new features that could be added to the original idea. At the end, we decided that “CoWork Central” is the idea that best suits the project’s rubric and has the most prospects for success while being implementable given the short timeline.

What makes our interface special?

The primary goal of this interface is to foster a profound sense of connection and collaboration among dispersed teams, recreating the ambiance, natural interactions and spontaneous encounters typically found within a physical office setting.

Key features that contribute to the interface’s uniqueness:

1. Real-time Presence and Activity Tracking: Our interface provides a comprehensive view of employees' activities, allowing remote workers to observe who is actively present at their desk, who is on a lunch break, using the washroom, or engaged in other activities within the virtual office space. This real-time presence tracking creates a sense of shared workspace despite physical distance.

2. Immersive Virtual Office Experience: The platform offers a visually immersive environment, enabling users to explore and navigate the virtual office space freely. The freedom of moving from one area to another promotes a more engaging and seamless experience that mimics the spontaneity and fluidity of walking around a physical office.

3. Proximity-Based Communication: By enabling communication based on virtual proximity, the interface replicates natural, spontaneous conversations, promoting collaboration and a sense of togetherness among remote employees.

4. User-Friendly Navigation: The interface is designed with an intuitive and user-friendly interface, allowing for easy navigation and interaction within the virtual office environment.

In summary, our interface redefines the remote work landscape by bridging the gap between physical separation and a cohesive, shared workspace experience through a virtual office setting that goes beyond conventional video conferencing tools. By replicating the dynamics of an in-person office setting, the interface offers an interactive and inclusive platform that fosters a sense of belonging and connection among remote employees that would eventually enhance productivity within distributed teams.
